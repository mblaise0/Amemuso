AMEMUSO Performing Arts Center (APAC)

Abuja City Will Never Be Thesame

Historically, a creatively flourishing artistic community supported by participation from the general public has been a clear sign of a healthy city. Ancient precedents for this phenomenon such as BCE Athens and 15th-century Florence, as well as modern cities like Newark (New Jersey), Pittsburgh and Cleveland in the United States, have all undergone significant revitalization periods following economic downturns. Revitalizations such as these often revolve around the creation or advancement of artistic initiatives, either publicly- or privately-funded (alongside other municipal-improvement programs). Art of any kind brings with it a certain cachet, a gleam of the modern that endows its owner with respectability and a reputation for being sought after that every city can appreciate

The AMEMUSO Performing Arts Center (APAC) and the Music School will have a dramatic and transformative impact on Abuja City, changing both the city's skyline as well the experirence of artists and the audiences throughout our region.The center itself is an 800-capacity state-of-the-art performance theatre. It is sure to become one of the signature architectural icons for Abuja, and be counted the finest performing arts centers in the world.

The technical requirements and the exacting standards required of a facility like the APAC make it one of the most complex structures to design to design and innovation and the technology to create virtually perfect acoustics and optimal sightlines in both performance halls.

APAC Foundation

The APAC will be constructed under a Build, Operate Transfer scheme as agreed by the AMEMUSO Board. This scheme will be implemented by the APAC foundation.
